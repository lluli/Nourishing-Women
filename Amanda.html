Carne Asada Tacos
Just as Amanda Gorman's poetry weaves together vivid imagery and powerful emotions, these Carne Asada Tacos blend bold flavors and vibrant ingredients to create a symphony of taste that will transport you to the bustling streets of Mexico.

Much like Gorman's ability to captivate an audience, this recipe embodies a perfect balance of textures and tastes. Juicy and tender grilled steak, marinated with a harmonious blend of citrus, garlic, and spices, takes center stage. Each bite carries the essence of Amanda Gorman's spirited presence and unyielding passion.

As Gorman's verses celebrate unity and resilience, these Carne Asada Tacos bring people together around the shared experience of savoring a delightful meal. With every bite, let the savory flavors awaken your senses and ignite a sense of togetherness and hope, just as Gorman's words have done for countless hearts.

Ingredients

STEAK
6 garlic cloves, finely chopped
1 bunch coarsely chopped cilantro
120ml cup fresh orange juice
120ml Mexican pale lager
80ml cup fresh lime juice
60ml extra-virgin olive oil
2 Tbsp. Diamond Crystal
1 Tbsp. freshly ground black pepper
2 tsp. apple cider vinegar
2 tsp. ground cumin
2 tsp. red chile powder
1kg skirt or flank steak, cut into 5"-long pieces

SALSA AND ASSEMBLY
3 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for grill
1 small white onion, halved
2 serrano chiles, stems removed
3 large tomatillos (or tomatoes)
3 garlic cloves, sliced
3 Tbsp. fresh lime juice
2½ tsp. Diamond Crystal
1 tsp. freshly ground black pepper
½ tsp. garlic powder
½ tsp. ground cumin
1 bunch chopped cilantro, plus more for serving

Flaky sea salt

Warm corn tortillas, chopped white onion, and lime wedges (for serving)

Method

Step One
Combine garlic, cilantro, orange juice, lager, lime juice, oil, salt, pepper, vinegar, cumin, and chile powder in a large baking dish. Add steak and turn to coat in marinade. Cover and chill, turning occasionally, at least 10 hours and up to 1 day.


Step Two
Prepare a grill for medium-high heat; oil grate. Grill onion halves, chiles, and tomatillos, turning often, until charred and tender, 10-15 minutes for onion and tomatillos, 8-12 minutes for chiles.


Step Three
Transfer vegetables to a blender and add garlic, lime juice, salt, pepper, garlic powder, cumin, 1 cup cilantro, and 3 Tbsp. oil. Carefully blend until salsa is mostly smooth (some texture is good but make sure there are no large chunks).


Step Four
Remove steak from marinade, letting excess drip back into dish (or bag); discard marinade. Grill steak, turning every minute or so, until charred and an instant-read thermometer inserted into thickest part registers 140° for medium, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a cutting board and let rest 10 minutes.


Step Five
Thinly slice steak against the grain, then cut into bite-size pieces. Transfer to a bowl and sprinkle with sea salt.


Step Six
Top tortillas with some steak, salsa, cilantro, and chopped onion. Serve with lime wedges for squeezing over and remaining salsa alongside.
