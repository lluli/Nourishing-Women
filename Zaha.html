Lemon olive oil cake
Just as Zaha Hadid pushed the boundaries of design and challenged conventions, this Lemon Olive Oil Cake pushes the boundaries of traditional baking to create a dessert that is as bold and unique as her architectural creations. The marriage of bright lemon and delicate olive oil creates a symphony of flavors that dance on your palate.

Hadid's groundbreaking architectural designs often featured bold curves and sleek lines, and this cake embodies that same sense of elegance and modernity. The tender crumb and citrusy notes of the cake are a testament to her ability to blend creativity with functionality.

With every forkful, let the vibrant lemon zest and the richness of olive oil transport you to the awe-inspiring world of Zaha Hadid's architectural wonders. Just as her designs evoke a sense of wonder and awe, this Lemon Olive Oil Cake will ignite your senses and leave you craving more.

Ingredients

Serves 6-8


1½ cups extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for pan

3 large eggs

1¼ cups (250 g) plus 1 Tbsp. granulated sugar

1¼ cups whole milk

2 tsp. vanilla extract

Zest and juice of 2 lemons; plus more zest for serving (optional)

2 cups (250 g) all-purpose flour

½ tsp. Morton kosher salt

½ tsp. baking powder

½ tsp. baking soda

Powdered sugar (for serving)

Method

Step One
Preheat oven to 350°. Lightly oil pan with a pastry brush. Line bottom with a parchment paper round and brush parchment with oil. Whisk eggs, 1½ cups oil, and 1¼ cups sugar in a medium bowl until smooth. Add milk, vanilla, and zest and juice of 2 lemons and whisk to combine.


Step Two
Whisk flour, salt, baking powder, and baking soda in a large bowl to combine. Make a well in the center and gradually pour egg mixture into well. Whisk, using circular motions to slowly incorporate egg mixture, until batter is mostly smooth (some lumps are okay). Scrape batter into prepared pan and smooth surface. Sprinkle remaining 1 Tbsp. sugar over.


Step Three
Bake cake until top is golden brown and a tester inserted into the center comes out clean, about 50 minutes. Transfer pan to a wire rack and let cake cool before unmolding. Sift in the flour and baking soda, then fold the mixture with a spatula (Be careful not to overmix, which would cause the gluten in the flour to toughen resulting in cakier cookies).


Step Four
Just before serving, dust cake with powdered sugar and top with more lemon zest if you have it.


Do ahead:
Cake can be baked 3 days ahead. Store tightly wrapped at room temperature.
